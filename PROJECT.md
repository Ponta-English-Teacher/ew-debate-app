# EW Debate App

## Purpose

EW Debate App is a visual argumentation platform for English language learning and critical thinking.

Unlike traditional debate, students are not assigned to fixed Pro and Con teams.

Students explore claims, challenge assumptions, provide evidence, ask questions, and develop arguments collaboratively.

The goal is to make thinking visible.

---

## Teacher Workflow

1. Select a topic (e.g., AI, Education, Social Media).
2. Work with AI to generate candidate motions.
3. Select 2–3 motions for the class.
4. Open the discussion board.
5. Flag important arguments using "Challenge This".
6. Generate AI summary at the end of the session.

---

## Student Workflow

1. Choose a motion.
2. Read existing argument cards.
3. Click a card to respond.
4. Choose response type:
   - Support
   - Counter
   - Challenge
   - Question
   - Evidence
   - Distinction
5. Submit response.
6. Vote for strong contributions.
7. Review personal contributions after class.

---

## Board Design

The board uses a Jamboard-style interface.

Each argument appears as a sticky-note card.

Cards are visually connected to the card they respond to.

Students can click a card to bring it to the foreground and respond.

The board should prioritize readability over density.

---

## AI Support

Teacher can enable or disable:

- How do you say it?
- Correct this
- Alternative expressions

AI does not debate for students.

AI supports language production only.

---

## Teacher Dashboard

Track:

- Post count
- Response count
- Vote count
- Word count
- Participation by response type

Teacher can view individual student contributions.

---

## End-of-Class Summary

AI generates:

- Main supporting arguments
- Main counterarguments
- Important challenges
- Unanswered questions
- Most popular arguments
- Strongest arguments
- Key insights from the discussion

---

## Educational Goal

Help students practice:

- Argumentation
- Critical thinking
- Discussion
- Academic writing
- Responding to others' ideas

The system should reward thoughtful engagement rather than winning debates.
